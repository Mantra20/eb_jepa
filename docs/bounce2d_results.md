# EB-JEPA × bounce2D — Project findings

## 1. Research question and gap in the literature

**Question:** *what physics — in the sense of conserved invariants (energy),
not merely instantaneous state variables (position, velocity) — is encoded in
a JEPA's latent, at which layer/processing stage, and is it **causally
necessary** to the model's behavior (not just correlated with it)?*

This formulation addresses a gap identified across three recent papers on
adjacent topics (review done during the "Hack the World(s)" hackathon,
June 2026):

| Paper | What it does | What it does not do |
|---|---|---|
| LeWM / LeCun (March 2026) | Probes a single model for emergent physical properties | No cross-architecture comparison, no causality test |
| "Probing the Latent World" (arXiv 2603.20327) | Probes instantaneous state variables in world-model latents | Does not test **conserved** invariants (energy, momentum) vs instantaneous state |
| Garrido et al. 2025 — V-JEPA intuitive physics | Evaluates "intuitive physics" capabilities of V-JEPA | Single model, no causal validation (ablation) of the controllability prior |

None of the three explicitly distinguishes "state variable readable from the
latent" from "conserved quantity encoded as such", and none provides causal
evidence (via ablation) that the detected physics is actually used by the
model rather than merely decodable after the fact. Phys-JEPA (arXiv,
June 15, 2026), published after the hackathon, is complementary rather than
competing: it **injects** physics explicitly into the architecture, whereas
this project **probes the spontaneous emergence** of physics in an
unconstrained JEPA.

## 2. Approach

Three axes of novelty relative to the state of the art above:

1. **Probe a conserved invariant** (kinetic energy `E = ½m‖v‖²`), not only
   instantaneous state variables — this requires a dedicated protocol, because
   a conserved quantity is *constant* within a trajectory and is therefore
   only probeable if it **varies across trajectories** (via randomized initial
   conditions).
2. **Compare several JEPA architectures** (Image/Video/AC-Video) on the same
   lib/env/data pipeline — not yet carried out for lack of post-hackathon
   time; currently limited to AC-Video-JEPA.
3. **Causal validation** by ablating the temporal-similarity regularization
   coefficient (`sim_coeff_t`), comparing a model trained with (Exp A) and
   without (Exp B) that term.

The proxy chosen to run this study quickly and in a controlled way is a
synthetic 2D environment (bounce2D) rather than a real physical dataset: the
ground truth (exact position, velocity and energy at every frame) is then
available without measurement noise or annotation ambiguity — a necessary
condition for the probes to measure the fidelity of the representation rather
than label noise.

## 3. Dataset generation and description

**Environment ([bounce2d/env.py](../bounce2d/env.py)):** a single ball in a
square box `[0,1]²`, bit-exact elastic bounces on the 4 walls (the velocity
sign flip at reflection preserves `v²` exactly, so `E` is conserved with no
numerical drift — essential for the ground truth to serve as a clean
reference). Fixed mass, `dt=1`. The momentum *vector* is **not** conserved in
a closed box (walls apply forces) — only energy is, which is why we probe `E`
rather than momentum.

**Randomized initial conditions:** uniform position in the box, speed with
uniform norm in `speed_range=(0.02, 0.10)` (range widened during the project
specifically to raise the coefficient of variation of energy across
trajectories — measured `CV(E_mean) ≈ 0.70`, a necessary condition for an
invariant conserved *within* a trajectory to remain probeable *across*
trajectories), uniform direction over `[0, 2π)`.

**Rendering ([bounce2d/render.py](../bounce2d/render.py)):** each frame is a
`65×65`, 2-channel image — channel 0: anti-aliased disc representing the ball
(soft edges rather than a binary mask, to prevent an encoder from trivially
learning a simple centroid); channel 1: static wall mask. No velocity or
energy is ever written into the image — the model receives a purely visual
signal, which guarantees that an energy signal detected in the latent stems
from inference by the model, not from information leakage in the input.

**Two distinct datasets, generated by the same engine:**
- **Training** (`build_dataset(paired=False)`): *normal* trajectories only —
  the world model must learn correct physics; it never sees a violation as a
  training input.
- **Evaluation** (`build_dataset(paired=True)`, `n_base=800`, dedicated seed
  `777`): each normal trajectory is paired with 4 *violated* variants,
  generated with the **same seed** up to the violation time `t_violation`
  (identical prefix) and diverging afterwards:
  - *Continuity* (discontinuous position, energy preserved): `teleport`
    (instantaneous position jump), `phantom_bounce` (passes through the wall
    instead of bouncing)
  - *Conservation* (continuous position, energy modified): `energy_gain`
    (`v *= 1.6`), `energy_loss` (`v /= 1.6`)

  This orthogonality (continuity vs conservation) is what makes the surprise
  test interpretable: a model that only tracks position should be surprised
  by `teleport`/`phantom_bounce` but **not** by `energy_gain`/`energy_loss` —
  any surprise measured on the latter, beyond the continuity reference, is
  evidence that the model encodes the conserved invariant and not just the
  instantaneous state.

**Why this dataset is fit for the question:** it deliberately isolates the
variable under study (energy — a *scalar*, *quadratic* function of velocity)
from any confound with position or the velocity vector, while providing exact
ground truth (no sensor noise), controlled across-trajectory variation, and a
violation suite built to distinguish "the model tracks position" from "the
model encodes a conserved quantity".

## 4. Parameters, experiments, and rationale

**Architecture (AC-Video-JEPA, `eb_jepa` — FAIR fork):** ImpalaEncoder
(2 input channels, latent output `D=512`), recurrent RNNPredictor, inverse
dynamics model (InverseDynamicsModel), `VC_IDM_Sim_Regularizer` combining
variance–covariance (VICReg-like), temporal similarity, and IDM terms.

**Two compared runs — the only manipulated variable is `sim_coeff_t`**
(temporal-similarity regularization coefficient, the core of the causal
ablation):

| | Exp A (`main_simt12_seed1`) | Exp B (`ablation_simt0_seed1`) |
|---|---|---|
| `sim_coeff_t` | 12 | 0 |
| Epochs | 300 | 100 (sufficient; convergence is faster without the regularization term) |
| `n_base` (train trajectories) | 4000 | 4000 |
| Batch size | 256 | 256 |
| `cov_coeff` / `std_coeff` | 12 / 16 (constant) | 12 / 16 (constant) |
| `idm_coeff` | 0 (IDM not weighted in the current loss — the causal ablation of the controllability prior via `L_IDM`, envisioned at the hackathon, has not yet been run on this pipeline) | same |
| Precision | bfloat16 + AMP | bfloat16 + AMP |

`sim_coeff_t=0` isolates the effect of the term that pushes temporally close
representations to be similar — hypothesis: without it, temporal collapse or
loss of the velocity/energy signal should show up in the probes, which tests
the causal necessity of the term for physics to emerge (rather than its mere
correlation with it).

**Anti-collapse monitoring during training:** `eff_rank` (spectral effective
rank of the representation covariance — healthy if `> 5`) and `temp_var`
(temporal variance — healthy if `> 0.1`), tracked every epoch to catch a
representational collapse before the probes come out spuriously negative.

## 5. Training, recalibration, train/test hygiene

**Training:** standard AC-Video-JEPA loop, checkpoints written to Google Drive
every 10 epochs (`e-{epoch}.pth.tar`), enabling a post-hoc sweep over multiple
checkpoints without retraining. Since Exp A's `latest.pth.tar` was corrupted
(interrupted write), all analyses use the last healthy checkpoint
`e-300.pth.tar`.

**Mid-project recalibration** — two significant corrective adjustments, both
documented for traceability:
1. **Widened speed range** `(0.02, 0.10)` — the initially tighter
   `speed_range` produced an energy signal with too little across-trajectory
   variance to be probed reliably (insufficient signal-to-noise ratio for the
   regression probes).
2. **Recalibrated ridge regularization** (`RidgeCV` on the grid
   `[1e-6, 1e2]` instead of a fixed `alpha=1.0`) in the secondary triviality
   probes (see §7) — the absolute scale of mean energy (`~1e-3`) made
   scikit-learn's default regularization completely unsuitable, regardless of
   the quality of the latent signal.

**Train/test splits:**
- **Model training:** train/val strictly separated at generation time
  (different seeds, `seed0=0` train vs `seed0=10_000_000` val); no trajectory
  leakage.
- **Official probes ([probes.py](../probes.py)):** split **by trajectory**
  (70/30, `_split_by_traj`), so no pair of frames from the same trajectory
  straddles the probes' train and test sets — necessary for R² to measure
  generalization to new trajectories rather than within-trajectory temporal
  interpolation.
- **Secondary triviality test (§7):** random 5-fold cross-validation over
  trajectories (IID by construction; a single observation per trajectory in
  this test).

## 6. Results — official probes (Exp A, e-300)

| Probe | Result | Reading |
|---|---|---|
| Position, instantaneous probe (1 frame) | R² = 0.999 | A single frame encodes position almost perfectly, as expected |
| Velocity, instantaneous probe | R² ≈ 0 (expected) | A single frame cannot contain motion information — serves as the reference floor |
| Velocity, temporal probe (pairs) | R² = 0.900 | Velocity is linearly decodable as soon as 2 consecutive frames are available |
| Energy, linear probe (pairs) | R² ≈ −0.056 | A linear combination of the latent does not decode energy |
| Energy, quadratic probe (PCA(24) + degree 2) | **R² = 0.750** | A quadratic combination of the latent decodes energy well — consistent with `E ∝ ‖v‖²` |
| Violation detection (post/pre surprise) | teleport ×7.46, phantom_bounce ×2.65, energy_gain ×1.54, energy_loss ×0.81 (not detected) | Strong continuity violations well detected; conservation violations detected asymmetrically — gain spotted, loss missed |

## 7. Results — secondary triviality test (is E encoded beyond v?)

Complements the official probe with a different question: at the
**trajectory** level (latent averaged over T, no prior PCA), is energy
decodable **beyond** what a linear reconstruction of speed would allow?
(Protocol and bug fixes — flat `gt_n` keys, 3-D `lat_n`, Jensen bias on the
mean vector — detailed in
[probing_energy_triviality.md](probing_energy_triviality.md).)

| Probe | R² |
|---|---|
| Direct quadratic (latent → E) | 0.570 |
| Direct linear (latent → E) | −0.254 |
| Indirect (latent → speed → Ê = ½·speed²) | −0.044 |
| **Delta (direct − indirect)** | **+0.644** |
| Permutation control (shuffled E) | −0.347 ± 0.054 |

The significant delta and the permutation control confirm that the
`R² = 0.570` signal is not an artifact of over-parameterization (131k
quadratic features for ~640 training trajectories).

**Methodological caution:** this `0.570` and the official `0.750` of §6 do
**not measure the same thing** — one operates per frame on pairs of
PCA(24)-reduced latents, the other at the trajectory level on the raw
time-averaged latent, without dimensionality reduction. The two results are
complementary, not replicates of a single measurement; they must be presented
as such in the final report.

## 8. Overall interpretation

Everything converges to a coherent reading across two independent
measurements (official probe §6 and secondary test §7): **kinetic energy is
encoded in the AC-Video-JEPA latent in a non-linear, non-trivial way**,
consistent with the hypothesis that the latent carries the velocity vector
and that energy is a quadratic read-out of it — not a quantity stored as an
independent scalar.

This result is a **necessary but not sufficient** condition for claiming a
"geometric representation of conservation" in the strong sense: a non-trivial
quadratic signal shows that energy is *derivable* from the latent, not that
the network learned an explicit concept of conservation as such. The
asymmetric detection of conservation violations (energy_gain detected,
energy_loss missed) points the same cautious way — the model's sensitivity to
the invariant is not uniform, which tempers a reading of "the model learned
energy conservation" in favor of a more measured one: "the model encodes
enough velocity information for energy to be derivable from it, with an
asymmetric sensitivity to violations".

**Open limitations, unresolved at this stage:**
- Cross-architecture comparison (Image/Video/AC-Video-JEPA): planned in the
  hackathon protocol, not yet run on bounce2D.
- Causal ablation of `L_IDM` (`idm_coeff=0` is already in place in the current
  run, but as a default configuration rather than a controlled A/B
  comparison): the proper causal test (with vs without, all else equal)
  remains to be run explicitly.
- The gap between `R² = 0.750` (official probe) and `R² = 0.570` (secondary
  test), while explained methodologically, has not been resolved by a unified
  protocol — a next pass could harmonize the two methods to produce a single
  reference number.
